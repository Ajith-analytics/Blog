crime

This dashboard provides a summary of crime data reported in Los Angeles from January 1, 2020, to July 3, 2023. The visualization highlights key aspects of the crime incidents, including the number of incidents, victim demographics, types of weapons used, and categories of crimes reported.

Key Insights: Total Crime Incidents: The dashboard displays a total of 752.91K crime incidents reported during the selected period.

Victim Demographics: Victim Age Distribution: The age group with the highest number of victims is 21-30 years, representing 24.95% of the total. Other significant age groups include 31-40 years (19.31%) and 41-50 years (13.06%).

Crime Status: The majority of the cases are classified under "Invest Cont" with 602.32K incidents. Other notable statuses include "Adult Other" (81.10K) and "Adult Arrest" (65.83K).

Weapon Use: The most common weapon used in these incidents is "Strong-Arm (Hands Fist Feet)" with 140K occurrences, followed by "Unknown Weapon" (27K) and "Verbal Threat" (19K). Firearms and other weapons like knives and pepper spray are also reported but in smaller numbers.

Crime Categories: The most frequent crime type is "Vehicle - Stolen" with 80K incidents, followed by "Battery - Simple Assault" (60K) and "Theft of Identity" (49K). Other common crimes include burglary, vandalism, and robbery.

verall Summary: The data reveals that a significant number of crimes in Los Angeles during this period involve young adults, particularly those aged 21-30 years. Males are slightly more likely to be victims than females. The most common crimes include vehicle theft, simple assault, and identity theft, with strong-arm assaults being the most frequently used method.
